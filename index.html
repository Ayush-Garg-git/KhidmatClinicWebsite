Will I be able to conceive soon?
Yes, with proper treatment and consistent follow-ups, conception is very much achievable for most patients.
Can I deliver normally after a C-section?
Yes, with a gap of 3.5 years and strict medical supervision, normal delivery after LSCS is absolutely possible.
Can I avoid a hysterectomy?
In many cases yes, timely treatment and identifying the root cause can delay or completely avoid surgery.
How long to recover after a C-section?
Full recovery ~3 weeks, but most mothers care for their baby independently within just 2–3 days.

Patient Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Real questions from real patients, answered honestly by Dr. Shazia Khan, Gynaecologist in Purnia, Bihar.

Yes, with proper treatment and regular follow-ups at Khidmat Healthcare, conception is very much possible. Every case is different, but with the right diagnostic approach and consistent treatment, most patients see positive results. Come in for a full evaluation and we will guide you step by step.
Yes, with a balanced diet, proper investigations, and regular prenatal counselling with Dr. Shazia Khan, a normal delivery without complications is achievable for most mothers. We monitor your pregnancy carefully and ensure the safest outcome for both you and your baby.
Yes, with the right treatment plan and following medical advice consistently, recovery is very much expected. Most patients notice significant improvement within weeks of starting treatment. Consistency with medicines, diet, and follow-up visits is the key to lasting recovery.
In many cases, yes. With timely treatment, regular checkups, and by identifying the root cause, a hysterectomy can often be delayed or completely avoided. Dr. Shazia Khan's approach is always treatment-first, surgery is recommended only when absolutely necessary and all other options have been exhausted.
Yes, VBAC (vaginal birth after caesarean) is possible, provided there is a gap of at least 3.5 years and it is conducted under strict medical supervision where emergency C-section facilities are available. This decision is made carefully after evaluating your individual case.
Yes, with appropriate treatment, a proper diet schedule, and targeted exercises for hormonal balance, menstrual irregularities can be resolved effectively. We first identify the cause through investigation, then design a personalised treatment plan for you.
Yes, but it requires a strict diet, proper medical treatment, and regular follow-up. Abdominal pain in women has multiple causes, we always assess the root cause before starting treatment to ensure lasting relief, not just temporary suppression of symptoms.
Full recovery takes approximately 3 weeks. However, most mothers are able to stand independently and care for their newborn within just 2–3 days of the procedure. With proper post-operative care, adequate rest, and good nutrition, recovery is smoother than most expect.
In certain conditions, yes, with proper ultrasound guidance, healthy baby status, adequate amniotic fluid levels, and appropriate cervical dilation. This is only done under careful supervision with emergency facilities available. Each case is individually assessed by Dr. Shazia Khan.
In most cases, yes, bleeding gradually resolves with the right medication, rest, and care. However, serious conditions like Placenta IBihareta require surgical intervention as they can be life-threatening. Any bleeding during pregnancy must be evaluated immediately at Khidmat Healthcare, please do not wait.
